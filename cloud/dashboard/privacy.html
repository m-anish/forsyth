The fine print, kept short

Privacy

A weather station is not a place to build a surveillance business. So we didn't.

No analytics, no trackers, no ads. Nothing on this page is counting you, profiling you, or selling you anything. There is no marketing pixel because there is no marketing.

Cookies: exactly one, and only if you're an admin who signs in — a session cookie so the server remembers you're logged in until you aren't. If you never sign in, you never get one.

Local storage: a single key that remembers whether you like the light or dark theme. It lives in your browser and goes nowhere.

Server logs: the machine keeps ordinary access logs — the boring who-fetched-what that every web server writes. They rotate away on their own; we don't mine them.

Third-party tiles: the map, terrain, and rain radar are fetched by your browser directly from OpenStreetMap / CARTO, OpenTopoMap, and RainViewer. Those services see your IP address, exactly as they would on any website that shows a map. We don't relay it and can't hide it — that's just how tiles work.

The weather data is the whole point. Temperatures, wind, rain, air quality — that's public, on purpose, for everyone. It's the product, not the payload.
